    THE CHESSBOARD

    What is the Chessboard?

    The chess board is made up of 64 squares (8x8) and is where chess is played on. There are 32 white squares and 32 black squares in a chequered pattern.

    Every square has a name and it deteremined by the file and rank of the square. Below will describe what files and ranks are along with diagonals figuring out the name of the square.


    Files

    Files are the vertical lines on the chessboard (8 squares long). Every file has a name and it is named by a letter. The letters range from a to h.

    It goes from the left, a, to the right, h in White's persepective. It goes from the left, h, to the right, a in Black's perspective.


    Ranks

    Ranks are the horizontal lines on the chessboard(8 squares long). Every Rank has a name and it it named by a number. The numbers range from 1 to 8.

    It goes from the bottom, 1, to the top, 8 in White's perspective. It goes from the bottom, 8, to the top, 1 in Black's perspective.


    Diagonals

    Diagonals are lines that join at 2 opposite squares. The diagonals are named by using their end squares.

    In the image above, the light-squared diagonal goes from a6-f1, vice versa. The dark-squared diagonal goes from e5-h8, vice versa.


    How to Determine the Name of A Square

    To determine the name of a square, we take the file name (letter) then the rank name (number).

    Example(s):

    The Name of the square that is on the e-file and the 4th-rank is: e4.

    The Name of the square that is on the c-file and the 7th-rank is: c7.
