    CHESS PIECES MOBILITY

    Pawn

    All pawns start on the 2nd rank for White or the 7th rank for Black. Both sides have 8 pawns in total.

    Pawns only move 1 square up at a time, however, on their very first move, they can move up to 2 squares up! Pawns can never move backwards.

    Pawns are also the only other piece that captures differently than how it moves. Pawns capture digaonally one square.

    See Image Above for reference.


    Rook

    All rooks start on the corners of the chessboard. a1 & h1 for White and a8 & h8 for Black. Both sides have 2 rooks in total.

    Rooks can move vertically and horizontally as far as they want as long as there is not another piece in the way.

    See Image Above for reference.


    Bishop

    Both sides have 2 bishops in total, each dark-squared or light-squared. For White, the bishops start on c1 and f1. For Black, the bishops start on c8 and f8.

    Bishops can move diagonally as far as they want as long as there is not another piece in the way. Bishops can NEVER switch the colored square that they are on. If a bishop is light-squared, then it stays on the light squares.

    See Image Above for reference.


    Knight

    Both sides have 2 knights in total. For White, the Knights start on b1 and g1. For Black, the knights start on b8 and g8.

    Knights move in an "L-shape" - move two squares in any direction vertically followed by one square horizontally. Vice versa, two squares in any direction horizontally followed by one square vertically.

    Knights are the ONLY pieces that can jump over other pieces.

    See Image Above for reference.


    Queen

    Both sides have 1 Queen. The most powerful piece in chess! For White, the Queen starts on d1. For Black, the Queen starts on d8.

    Queens can move vertically, horizontally, and diagonally. The Queen can move freely as far as she wants as long as there is not another piece in the way.

    Think of the Queen as the combination of both the Rook & Bishop together.

    See Image Above for reference.


    King

    Both sides have 1 King. The most important piece in chess! For White, the King starts on e1. For Black, the King starts on e8.

    The King can move in all directions(vertically, horizontally, and diagonally) BUT only 1 square at a time.
