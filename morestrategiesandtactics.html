    More Strategies & Tactics

    Prophylaxis

    Prophylaxis is a move or series of moves to prevent the opponent from taking some action/following with their plan.

    White has a tactic on Black, a pin! White plans to put more pressure on the pin by playing Rook to e1.

    However, if White does that, then the Black Knight will capture the Knight on f3 with check! Escaping the trouble as White has to react to the check.

    That is why White plays the move King to h1. Black can no longer take on f3 as it is not a check and White will capture the Black Queen on c7 with the Bishop.

    White can now then continue with the plan to pressuring the pin by playing Rook to e1.


    Sacrifice

    A Sacrifice is when one player gives up a piece, either for nothing or in exchange for one of lesser value, in return for some other advantage.

    The White Queen will go to b8, sacrificing itself. Black is forced to capture the Queen with the Knight on d7.

    White lost a whole queen, but in return, White can checkmate Black by playing Rook to d8.


    Zugzwang

    Zugzwang is an event where the opponent must make a move and no matter what they move, it will be bad.

    The White Rook will go to a6 and no matter what Black does, Black will lose.

    If Black takes the White Rook with the b-pawn, then White will play Pawn to b7 Checkmate!

    If Black moves their Bishop to any square, then the White Rook will take on a7 Checkmate!
