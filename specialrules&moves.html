    Special Rules & Moves

    Castling

    Castling is the only time in chess where two pieces can be moved at once. The main purpose of castling is to provide King safety. You can castle either Queenside(longside) or Kingside(shortside).

    To castle, the King has to move two squares to the left or right and Rook moves over right next to the King.

    You CANNOT castle if there are pieces in the way of the King or Rook to move. You CANNOT castle if your King is in check or walk into check.

    You CANNOT castle if you have already moved your King once or moved a Rook that you wanted to castle with.

    Pawn Promotion

    Pawn Promotion is a move that allow one to change the pawn into a much powerful piece when it reached the end of the board.

    The end of the board for a White Pawn is the 8th rank. The end of the board for a Black Pawn is the 1st rank.

    A Pawn can promote to either a Bishop, Knight, Rook, or Queen. A Pawn CANNOT promote to a Pawn or King!

    En Passant

    En Passant is a special move that can occur when a pawn passes another pawn, in which the pawn that was passed can capture the opposing pawn.

    In other words, if a pawn moves two squares up and lands right next to your pawn, then your pawn can capture that pawn by going one square behind the passed pawn.

    You ONLY get one chance to play En Passant if allowed, once another turn from your opponent occurs, then the opportunity to En Passant is lost.

    Check

    A Check is a move that puts the King in danger. If a King is in check, there are 3 options to get out of check: move away from the check, capture the check, or block the check.

    Checkmate

    A Checkmate is a move that puts the King in danger and the King cannot move, causing the end of the game.
