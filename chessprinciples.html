    Chess Principles

    Controling the Center

    The 1st Chess Principle is to Control the Center!

    The most important area of the chessboard is the center. The center consist of the squares: e4, d4, e5, and d5. It is essential to have control of the center so your pieces can have mobility or space to move around.

    If there is no control over the center, then your position becomes passive which can lead to trouble, opponent attacking.

    In order to control the center, either place pieces directly on the center or have pieces control the center/can move on the center.

    Developing Pieces

    The 2nd Chess Principle is to Develop Your Pieces!

    You need to bring your pieces out to the party! You can't let your pieces be sitting ducks!

    To develop your pieces, you want to bring out all your minor pieces, the knights and bishops, towards the center. The closer your minor pieces are towards the center, then the more control they have on the center.

    King Safety

    The 3rd Chess Principle is King Safety!

    It is important to protect your King as its value is the whole game! If the King is checkmated, then the game is over.

    The center is where all the action occurs, you don't want your King to be caught up in the action! To get your King to safety, you perform a special move called Castling!

    Castling involves moving the King 2 squares then moving the Rook over next to the King.
